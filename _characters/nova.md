Nova Jocelyn Buckley was born at 11:43pm on December 31st, 1998 in Los Angeles, California. She is the second child and only daughter of well-known director Harvey Buckley and fashion designer Octavia Abbott. Her brother, Arden Abbott-Buckley, is three years her senior. With her parents’ high profile careers, all eyes were on Nova from the very beginning.

When Nova was a child, Harvey would often joke that her resistance to being born in the new year was an indication of the stubborn, ambitious girl his daughter would become. From the time she learned to walk, Nova never let anything stop her. If anything, she lived her life with the idea that she had to be the best at anything she did. Anything Arden could do, Nova strived to do it bigger and better. Whether that was writing short stories, climbing treats, or eating a hotter hot sauce, Nova did things big — and she did big things well.

Throughout her childhood, Nova was closest with her father, often choosing to spend time with him on sets whenever she could. Harvey noticed the ambition and passion for film in his daughter and often encouraged it. The same could not be said for her mother. Octavia openly preferred her son and rarely hid her favoritism of Arden over Nova — especially when Nova showed no interest in being a model for her fashion brand.

Her parents, both more devoted to their work than each other, divorced when Nova was eight years old. Their divorce was highly publicized — especially by Octavia, believing it would gain her favor in court. Harvey attempted to keep the children out of the media frenzy of it, but when Octavia put Nova on the stand to publicly choose between her parents, it was impossible for them to not be a target of the tabloids.

Ultimately, between Nova’s tearful words in the judge’s private chambers and Octavia’s actions, Harvey got custody of Nova while Arden — who openly chose their mother — primarily lived with Octavia. While Nova saw Octavia on occasion, her relationship with her mother and brother never truly recovered. Even now, their relationship is estranged — at best.

Already homeschooled, Nova spent even more time on set with her father, traveling with him as much as she could. When she couldn’t, she would stay with her longtime friend, Camie Taylor, whose father was also a director and supported Harvey as much as he could. Nova never minded being on set. If anything, she wanted to be exactly like her father. She’d already started writing from a young age and was known for her tendencies to “direct” other children, especially Camie, in play to act out her creations.

It was no surprise that Nova wanted to go into film, and her father was delighted when she got into UCLA. She obtained both her Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees from the university, all while working on sets as much as she could. By the time she’d graduated with her Bachelor’s, she was already establishing a name for herself, and by the time she completed her Master’s, she was writing significant pieces of a popular CW show.

All the while, Nova had been working on her baby: what would become the queer Netflix drama, When Stars Collide. While working on other sets, she pieced together the first four episodes in completion, even self-filming a few scenes with her friend and actress Mena Guerrero, who would later become the lead in the show. When a friend of her father’s asked what she had been working on at her father’s birthday party, she happily showed him clips she’d recorded — just for fun.

Nova thought nothing of the interaction until, a few months after her graduation, she was approached by a producer at Netflix with an interest in developing it into a full show. Realizing it as an opportunity to merge her two passions — writing and directing — Nova negotiated to become not just the writer, but the showrunner and director as well. It was a bold move, but it paid off. Netflix accepted the deal, and she immediately cast Mena Guerrero as the lead.

But just like everything else she’s done, Nova has no intention of giving up.
