In her own opinion, Charlie Mackenzie’s life really began on the day that Adria entered her world.

What came before hardly counts at all.

Charlotte “Charlie” Rose Mackenzie was born on July 13th, 2000 in Medford, Oregon to Dr. Christopher Mackenzie, a heart surgeon, and Dr. Michelle Mackenzie, a neonatal surgeon. Even from her early years, Charlie was close with her mother while her father was highly critical of her whenever he actually took interest in her.

According to her mother and uncle, Charlie was an easy child. She was bright, curious, and often lost in her own thoughts. Though she wouldn’t receive an autism diagnosis until much later, the signs were always there. To her mother, those traits – her nearly photographic memory, the way she thrived on patterns and routine, the inquisitive way she viewed the world – were the best parts about her. To her father, they were oddities that made his daughter less than perfect.

Charlie’s life really began when she was five years old. One winter day, while she and her mother were driving back from dropping her uncle at the airport, they witnessed a car accident that would change the rest of their lives. Michelle pulled over to help, telling Charlie to cover her eyes and not get out of the car – something she easily obeyed. There was no saving the two adults in the car or the other child, but with Michelle’s help, they were able to stabilize the little girl in the backseat until they could get to the hospital, where Michelle and Christopher performed surgery that saved her life.

That little girl was Adria Benson.

Michelle couldn’t stand the idea of the child being alone in the hospital after everything she’d gone through. She stayed with her, even bringing Charlie in to meet Adria as soon as she was fully stable. From that moment on, it was clear to anyone who met them that they were destined to be sisters. Five months apart in age, the pair had identical ocean blue eyes that made strangers mistake them as twins and a bond that couldn’t be broken. When it became clear that Adria had no remaining family left, the Mackenzies fostered and later adopted her, making their sisterhood official.

Growing up, Adria’s health was fragile. Though she had survived the accident, it had left a myriad of lasting heart conditions. Charlie’s childhood and teenage years were often spent in hospitals, curling up at Adria’s bedside whenever her sister was too sick to be at home. Charlie never minded. She would have always rather been with Adria than without her. Christopher treated Adria like glass due to her condition, while Charlie and Michelle refused to let her be defined only by her illness. Charlie often snuck her out of the house late at night to go sledding when it snowed or had secret dance parties late at night. She refused to let Adria miss out on a childhood because of what she had gone through.

While Christopher treated Adria like glass, the same didn’t apply to Charlie. Always observant, she already understood what happened behind closed doors and how her father treated her mother. As she grew, that abuse shifted towards her as well. He’d always been cold and critical with her, but soon things progressed past sharp words whenever Charlie didn’t meet his impossible standards. She learned to hide bruises and minimize impact, though Adria and Michelle saw through it all. His abuse was constant to Michelle and Charlie, and though he never raised a hand to Adria no matter how often she tried to take the blame off her sister, the Mackenzie household felt like walking on a minefield.

Everything changed when Charlie and Adria were fifteen. Charlie doesn’t talk about that night, but Christopher’s abuse took a particularly violent turn that made Michelle finally confess what was happening to her twin brother, Devon. He stepped in immediately, paying for lawyers that helped with custody and gave them refuge in Boston, where he lived. The change was a breath of fresh air for them all – they finally had a fresh start somewhere safe. Adria had incredible doctors in Boston to help with her health, and Charlie finally received the autism diagnosis Christopher refused to let Michelle seek for their daughter. In Boston, things were safe, stable, and normal for the first time in their life.

When it came time for college, there was no question that Adria and Charlie would remain near each other. They researched colleges together, eventually agreeing to move to New York City. They both enrolled in school – Adria to study Marine Biology while Charlie chose to study architecture. Neither of them wanted to live in dorms, instead choosing to live together in a two bedroom apartment near campus. Just as they always had, they supported each other through college and into graduate school.

Now, with their degrees complete, neither of them have wanted to move away from each other. They still share an apartment as they’ve progressed into their adult lives and careers. Adria works full time at the aquarium, pursuing her dream career, while Charlie is working the million supervised hours as a junior architect to get fully licensed. It’s busy, sometimes chaotic, but the kind of life that Charlie has always wanted to live – with Adria, her best friend, sister, and other half.
