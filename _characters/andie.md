Andromeda “Andie” Lillian Collins was born on February 3rd, 1991 in Chicago, IL. She was the first daughter of Elliot, a carpenter, and Allison, a music teacher. Her younger sister, Thalia Rachel Collins, was born five years later. Growing up, she was very close with her family.

When Andie was seven, she lost her mother when a drunk driver ran a stoplight and plowed into her mother’s car. Heartbroken over the loss of his wife, her father never remarried. Instead, he focused on being a good parent to his children and enlisted his family’s help in raising his daughters. Despite his heavy work load, he never missed a parent teacher conference and encourage creativity and passion within his daughters.

Andie was an exceptional student throughout high school, which earned her a scholarship to Oxford University. Despite her initial reluctance to leave her father at home with her teenage sister, both Elliot and Thalia told her they’d never forgive her if she didn’t take the opportunity. So Andie accepted a spot at St. Anne’s College at Oxford University, where she studied English Literature.

On her first night of university, Andie met Tamzin Reynolds, another student of St. Anne’s College. Their rooms were next door to each other, and Andie couldn’t help but press her ear to their shared wall to hear the music Tamzin played. It only took a few hours for her to knock on Tamzin’s door, and from there, her life was forever changed.

Over time, their friendship became something more. The first time they kissed, Andie swore she never wanted to kiss anyone else ever again. Fortunately for her, Tamzin felt the same. From that moment on, Andie’s heart belonged to Tamzin.

After obtaining her Bachelor’s Degree, Andie continued at St. Anne’s College to obtain both her Master of Studies (MSt) and Doctor of Philosophy (DPhil) in English Literature. All the while, she lived with and studied with Tamzin, knowing that she wanted to be with her forever, even if having to return to the US loomed over them.

On the day of their graduation, Tamzin surprised her with the best gift she’d ever received: Tamzin’s own job offer to teach at Columbia College Chicago. At first, she’d stared at the letter and thought it was too good to be true, but Tamzin reassured her that her future was with Andie, wherever that would be.

Not long after moving to Chicago, Tamzin proposed to Andie in Millennium Park. She’d asked her to meet her near the Bean, a favorite spot for them to meet for lunch. The proposal was perfect. Just the two of them with rings that had once belonged to their mothers and a question that Andie had already answered in her heart a hundred times. It was the easiest “yes” of her life. They married the following April in a small ceremony surrounded by their closest friends and family - with Thalia as her maid of honor and her father proudly walking her down the aisle.

Two years later, they welcomed their daughter, Eden Madeline Reynolds, into their family. She had Tamzin’s eyes and Andie’s impossibly stubborn personality, and from the first moment they held her, everything changed. Parenthood wasn’t always easy, but it was always filled with joy, laughter, and late nights laying in bed with Eden nestled between them. Their little family was exactly what they’d always dreamed of and Andie wouldn’t have asked for anything better.
