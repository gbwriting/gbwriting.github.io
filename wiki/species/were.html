Weres

Weres

Classification Supernatural
Mortality Mortal
Lifespan 130 - 140 Years
Threat Level Medium
Groups Packs

Weres are supernatural beings born with the spirit of an animal inside of them. This connection shapes their instincts, senses, and physical abilities from the moment they are born. Their magic is deeply tied to the moon, with lunar cycles demanding a shift at each Full Moon. A Were’s first shift typically occurs in early childhood, and while the experience can be frightening or disorienting, it becomes easier to manage with age.

By puberty, most Weres can control their shifts and do so at will regardless of the moon cycle. However, shifting during each full moon remains non-negotiable. Resisting it is excruciating — and in some cases, dangerous. Their body temperature runs naturally higher than that of humans or other supernaturals, typically around 99°F, and climbs steadily as the full moon approaches, remaining elevated until they finally shift into their animal form.

Even in their human form, Weres are stronger than typical humans. Their heightened senses, increased physical strength and stamina, and sharpened reflexes differentiate them from other beings. When they shift, these traits intensify. In their animal form, Weres gain all the strengths, weaknesses, and natural characteristics of the creature they become — all while retaining their human mind beneath it.

History

Weres have existed for as long as anyone can remember, though their true origin is lost to history. There are numerous pieces in folklore attempting to explain their beginnings, all ranging in where the credit for their creation is given. Some accounts speak of a curse cast by ancient Witches, others believing they were created by the Fae Queen herself, while others whisper of a moon goddess who tied their souls to her light.

Over time, these stories have transformed into regional mythologies, with each culture or region telling its own version of how Weres came to be. Of course, none of these legends have ever been proven, but they’ve shaped the way humans perceive Weres. Modern media has only amplified these narratives, sensationalizing them as monsters or heroes while overlooking the complexity of their actual lives.

In the present day, Weres live ordinary lives among humans, working in every field — from medicine to politics to high profile celebrities. They simply do so with caution and discretion. Because their supernatural side can draw unwanted attention and danger, most Weres learn early on to blend into human society without suppressing who they really are.

Abilities, Strengths, & Weaknesses

Despite media portrayals of dramatic werewolf bites, Weres are born, not turned. Their supernatural nature is inherited directly from their parents. A child of a Were will always be born Were, regardless of the presence of other supernatural species in their bloodline. Each Were inherits their animal form from one parent and it is never a hybrid of their parents’ species. These forms are tied exclusively to real world animals — no Were has ever been known to shift into an aquatic, amphibious, or mythological creature.

Weres are deeply connected to the moon, with its cycles governing their shifts. Their first transformation typically occurs in early childhood during a full moon, a process that can be disorienting or painful. By puberty, most Weres have learned how to control their shifts regardless of the moon cycle. However, shifting at the full moon remains non-negotiable. As it approaches, their already heightened body temperature continues to rise steadily and remains elevated until they transform into their animal form.

Even in their human form, Weres are stronger, possessing heightened senses, increased strength and stamina, and quick reflexes that set them apart from humans and supernaturals alike. They tend to be resilient, healing quickly from injuries and being less susceptible to common illnesses. However, their enhanced physiology comes with a need for additional food and nutrition to maintain their strength and energy. Weres may also develop secondary supernatural abilities, often dependent on their personality.

When shifted, Weres take on the full physical traits and characteristics of the animal they become. Their senses — sight, smell, hearing, and reflexes — are amplified to match their species. While shifted, they gain the ability to communicate telepathically with other Weres or individuals with psychic abilities. Even in this form, their human mind remains in control, guiding their actions and making them uniquely powerful.

Strengths

  • Fast Healing Weres recover from injury significantly faster than humans. Broken bones mend in a matter of days while cuts can heal in hours or days depending on severity. Silver-inflicted wounds, however, take significantly longer to heal from.
  • Enhanced Senses: Their senses of smell, hearing, and sight are heightened, even while in human form.
  • Resilient: Weres rarely fall ill from human illnesses and recover quickly when they do. Their bodies heal quickly and it takes significantly more to knock them off their feet.

Weaknesses

  • Silver: Silver is extremely dangerous to a Were. Diluted silver may only irritate or burn them, while large quantities can result in injury or death.
  • Increased Hunger: Weres have an increased metabolism and require more food than humans to sustain their bodies, especially during a full moon.
  • Body Temperature: Weres have an increased body temperature of 99°F, increasing near the full moon, which can be noticeable to others.

Lifespan & Mortality

Weres are mortal, but their supernatural physiology grants them significantly longer lives than humans. Most live between 130 to 140 years, though may live longer if exceptionally healthy. Weres begin to age slower as they hit their mid-20s, remaining physically young and capable far longer than their human counterparts.

Typically, Weres die from old age, significant illness, or a dangerous injury. While Weres are stronger and more difficult to injure, they remain mortal and can be killed.

Reproduction & Lineage

Children of Weres are always born as Weres, with no known exceptions. Even if there are other supernatural species in the bloodline, the Were gene is dominant and will always be present in the child of a Were.

Groups

Weres organize themselves differently depending on their environment and personality.

In more rural or secluded areas, traditional packs are common. These packs are led by an Alpha and their Beta and tend to include animal forms that are closely tied or related to each other. These packs provide community, stability, and protection.

In cities, Weres may have packs disguised as gangs or other large groups. However, it is more common for Weres to live more independently or in small family units. As their supernatural nature makes them easier to identify than other supernaturals, many stick to smaller or quieter groups to minimize the risk of detection.

Additionally, a Were may become the Familiar of a Witch. This bond offers additional protections to both parties. However, this connection is not simple and cannot be forced. The ritual to connect a Were and Witch requires a deep emotional connection in order to succeed, making it a rare connection.
