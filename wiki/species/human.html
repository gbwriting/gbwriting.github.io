Humans are fully mortal beings characterized by a complete lack of supernatural traits or abilities. Making up the vast majority of the world’s population, they outnumber every supernatural species by an overwhelming margin. Human societies, governments, cultures, and technologies control the modern world, often shaping the environment and enacting laws that affect supernaturals far more than the average human realizes. As a species, humans tend to believe only what they can observe, leaning much more heavily on science than magic.

While select groups are aware of the magic existing in the world, the general populace spends their lives entirely ignorant of the supernatural communities living alongside them. Certain families have always known about the existence of supernatural creatures, passing this truth down through generations. However, the average human has no concept of real magic, viewing it only through the lens of myths, folklore, and fictional media.

In certain regions, however, awareness of the supernatural is becoming more common, driven either by personal encounters or growing belief. Some areas are actively protected by magic to create safe havens for supernaturals. These regions naturally draw a higher population of humans who know the truth—though not all of them believe the world is better off coexisting with supernatural entities.

History

Though origin stories vary across cultures, humans have inhabited Earth for thousands of years, existing long before supernatural species emerged—or at least, before they became widely known. Throughout history, encounters between humans and supernatural beings have regularly surfaced in folklore, legends, and religious texts, though these accounts were historically dismissed as pure fiction. Driven by this skepticism, humanity shaped its civilizations based entirely on what could be physically seen, measured, and explained.

This tendency remains largely unchanged in the modern era. The vast majority of humans live and die without ever realizing magic is woven into the world around them. The tiny minority who do hold this knowledge—whether through lineage, personal experience, or proximity to supernatural sanctuaries—typically keep quiet to protect the supernatural community from exposure and harm.

As modern safe havens expand, human awareness is slowly tracking upward. These specific areas see higher concentrations of humans who either know the truth or strongly suspect it. However, awareness does not always equal acceptance; human reactions run the full spectrum, ranging from curiosity and fear to outright hostility and hatred. Despite these localized shifts, the global human population remains largely oblivious to the magical world.

Abilities, Strengths, & Weaknesses

Humans possess no innate supernatural traits or magical capabilities, and they cannot wield magic on their own. However, certain individuals do display localized sensitivities to magical environments or supernatural presence. Furthermore, a lack of personal magic does not equate to immunity; humans are highly susceptible to spells, curses, and alchemical tonics, and those who are aware of magic will often pay an incredibly high price to use it to their own advantage.

Strengths

  • Anomalous Safety: Lacking a magical aura makes them less visible to hidden threats and safe from forces that specifically target supernatural entities.
  • Societal Power: Holding absolute control over global infrastructure allows human institutions to create laws that dictate the baseline survival of supernatural populations.

Weaknesses

  • Physical Fragility: Human bodies are highly susceptible to standard illnesses, organic diseases, and conventional physical injuries.
  • Inherent Ignorance: Total unawareness of the supernatural populace leaves the average human completely blind to hidden magical dangers.
  • Metaphysical Vulnerability: Lacking natural magical defenses makes humans exceptionally easy targets for magical manipulation, mental influence, or supernatural attack.

Lifespan & Mortality

Humans have average lifespans, typically living between 80 to 90 years, though some individuals may live longer than that under exceptional health and circumstance. They age steadily, experience physical decline, and are vulnerable to disease, injury, and other dangers – supernatural or not.

Their mortality is one of their greatest limitations, but also the source of their greatest motivation. Driven by the idea of legacy, humans often act quickly, make the most out of limited time, and try to create things that exist after they are gone.

Reproduction & Lineage

While humans typically produce human offspring, they are biologically compatible with various supernatural species. In hybrid pairings, the child's species is governed by specific hereditary rules:

  • Hunters: Pairings between a human and a Hunter invariably result in a standard human child.
  • Weres: Genetic traits for Weres are completely dominant. A child born to a human and a Were will always inherit full Were status, with no known exceptions.
  • Witches: Genetic traits for Witches are also completely dominant. A child born to a human and a Witch will always be a Witch themselves.
  • Fae: Pairings with the Fae are highly unpredictable. In the vast majority of cases, the child will be born entirely human. A Fae child is only possible if both the human and Fae parent carry latent lineages directly tied to the original, ancient Fae bloodlines—an occurrence that remains exceptionally rare.
